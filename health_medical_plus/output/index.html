A natural molecule boosts CAR-T therapy and turns cold tumors hot

CAR-T cell therapy works well in blood cancers, but many patients still become resistant. A key reason is the presence of CAR-T regulatory T cells (CAR-Tregs), which weaken immune responses. Therefore, selectively targeting CAR-Tregs while preserving CAR-T activity remains a major challenge. Now, a pharmaceutical research team has identified a natural compound, timosaponin AIII (TAIII), that selectively eliminates CAR-T regulatory T cells. The study is published in Nature Communications.

天然分子がCAR-T療法を促進し、冷たい腫瘍を熱くする

CAR-T 細胞療法は血液がんに効果がありますが、依然として多くの患者が耐性を示しています。主な理由は、免疫応答を弱めるCAR-T制御性T細胞（CAR-Tr​​eg）の存在です。したがって、CAR-T 活性を維持しながら CAR-Tr​​eg を選択的に標的にすることは依然として大きな課題です。今回、製薬研究チームは、CAR-T制御性T細胞を選択的に除去する天然化合物チモサポニンAIII（TAIII）を特定した。この研究はNature Communications誌に掲載されている。

Revealing the unusual ability of a protein involved in lung and thyroid cancer

Research conducted at the National Cancer Research Centre (CNIO) has revealed an unexpected behavior observed in a protein involved in several types of cancer: it manages to self-activate, meaning it gives itself the order to start working in the cell. This finding shows that its activation process is accelerated, making it much faster than in normal proteins.
